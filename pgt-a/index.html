PGT-A

PGT-A

OVERVIEW

PGT-A, or preimplantation genetic testing for aneuploidies, is a genetic test performed on embryos before transfer. PGT-A tests for chromosome abnormalities that decrease the likelihood of a pregnancy. Embryos free of a chromosomal aneuploidy are transferred resulting in a higher pregnancy rate. PGT-A was formerly known as PGS, preimplantation genetic screening.

The normal number of chromosomes in humans is 46. PGT-A looks at the number of chromosomes within an embryo. If an embryo has more or less than 46 chromosomes, the chance of a successful pregnancy is decreased. Using PGT-A to determine whether or not an embryo has the correct number of chromosomes increases the chance of a successful pregnancy.

PGT-A has the potential to improve pregnancy outcomes in the following ways:

  • Increased pregnancy, ongoing pregnancy and live birth rates
  • Decreased miscarriage risk
  • More confidence in single embryo transfer (SET)
  • Reduction in time needed to achieve pregnancy

  • Embryos with mosaicism will be reported out as either aneuploid or euploid based on the percentage of mosaicism in the biopsy. The higher the level of mosaicism, the more likely the embryo will be called aneuploid.
