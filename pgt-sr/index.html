PGT-SR

PGT-SR

OVERVIEW

PGT-SR, or preimplantation genetic testing for structural rearrangements is for individuals with a chromosomal rearrangement, such as a balanced translocation or an inversion. Genetic testing is performed on the embryo biopsy to determine which embryos have an incorrect amount of genetic material (unbalanced). These embryos are typically not selected for transfer. At Genomic Prediction, we can differentiate between embryos which are carriers of the balanced chromosomal rearrangement and those with normal chromosomes people even more options when choosing which embryos to transfer. PGT-SR was formerly known as PGD, preimplantation genetic diagnosis.

PROCESS

  1. Case Submission: Through our provider portal, medical records are submitted for review by our Genomic Prediction Clinical Laboratory team to determine if PGT-M can be performed and if other family member samples are needed.
  2. Genetic Counseling:
    1. If the case is approved, a genetic counseling appointment to discuss the benefits and limitations of PGT-SR will be scheduled.
    2. Our genetic counselor will facilitate collection of parental samples and any necessary family samples.
    3. To learn more about our Genetic Counseling services, click here.
  3. Embryo Biopsy and Reporting:
    1. Once the parental samples are received in the Genomic Prediction Clinical Laboratory, biopsies can be sent for testing.
    2. Embryos created without intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) are subject to DNA contamination by other sperm, and cannot be accepted for PGT-SR testing.
    3. PGT-SR is performed and results are available within two weeks in our provider portal.
    4. PGT-A to address aneuploidy unrelated to the parental rearrangement is included at no additional charge.

RESULTS

  1. Negative: A negative result indicates that the usual number of chromosomes in the usual order was detected.
  2. Balanced: This result indicates that the balanced chromosome rearrangement identified in the parent is also identified in the sample.
  3. Unbalanced: This results indicates that extra or missing chromosome material related to the parental chromosome rearrangement was detected.
  4. Positive: A positive result indicates the sample has extra or missing chromosome material unrelated to the parental chromosome rearrangement.
